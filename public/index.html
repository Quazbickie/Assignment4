Simple Quiche

A quiche is a classic, versatile crowd pleaser. Perfect for breakfast, lunch or dinner, this simple recipe will allow you to create a quiche in under an hour. It makes a lovely main dish or is great for grabbing a slice while working from home. Allow your creativity to takeover and alter the recipe to your taste.

Time Required 55 Minutes
Difficulty Easy to Medium
  • For Pastry:
  • Plain Cream Flour 210 grams
  • Cold Unsalted Butter 100 grams
  • Cold Water 6 tablespoons
  • For Filling:
  • Large Eggs 2 eggs
  • Single Cream 250 mls
  • Gruyere 125 grams
  • Cheddar and Mozzarella Mix 50 grams
  • Onion 1 large
  • Tomato 2 tomatos
  • Spinach 50 grams
  • Diced Ham 100 grams
  • Salt and Pepper A pinch
  • For Pastry:
    • Dice the butter into small cubes and combine with flour in a large mixing bowl. Mix gently with fingers until roughly the texture of fine breadcrumbs.
    • Once at desired texture, slowly add tablespoons of cold water until the pastry dough comes together. Be patient and have faith.
    • When dough has been combined into small ball, roll out on lightly floured surface. Roll dough until it is roughly 11 inches in diameter, and large enough to cover tart tin base and sides, with enough overhang so you can tightly fit the dough to your tin.
    • Preheat oven to 220°C. Tightly place dough into tin bottom and sides, and allow to rest in fridge for 20 minutes.
    • Gently pierce dough bottom with fork to prevent it from rising in oven. Be very careful not to go entirely through dough. Cover pastry bottom and sides with baking paper and weigh down with baking beans. If you don't have baking bean, plain rice or beans will suffice, but be sure to use enough to really weigh the dough down.
    • Bake for 10 minutes. Remove baking paper and baking beans from pastry and bake for a further 10 minutes. Allow to cool before adding filling.
  • For Filling:
    • Combine eggs and cream in a large bowl and mix well. Add salt and pepper to taste.
    • Dice onion and tomato. Lightly brown onion in pan, and then add onion and tomato to pastry base.
    • Add grated gruyere, diced ham and spinach leaves to pastry base.
    • Pour egg mix into pastry base and sprinkle cheddar and mozzarella, as well as any left over filling items on top.
    • Place in oven preheated to 180°C and bake for 25-30 minutes.
